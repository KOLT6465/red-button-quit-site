Red Button Quit

Support

Straightforward answers for setup, permissions, and app compatibility.

Get started

  1. Open Red Button Quit. Its icon appears in the menu bar.
  2. Choose the icon, then select Enable.
  3. Allow the requested macOS permission.
  4. Open one window in an enabled app and click its red close button.

Common questions

Why did the app stay open?

Red Button Quit acts only when it recognizes a safe close. Another window, a minimized window, full screen, a save alert, a disabled app, or an unusual window layout can leave the app's normal behavior unchanged.

Which apps work?

Choose Supported Apps from the menu bar. Verified apps are ready by default. You can enable other apps as Not Verified, although they may not work with every window.

Will Red Button Quit discard unsaved work?

No. It sends the app's normal Quit command. The app's own save and cancellation alerts remain in control.

How do I check permission?

Open System Settings, choose Privacy & Security, then Accessibility. Make sure Red Button Quit is enabled. Return to the menu bar and choose Enable once.

Why is an app marked Not Verified?

It has not completed the full Red Button Quit compatibility test. It stays off until you choose to try it, and all one-window safety checks still apply.

Does Red Button Quit collect data?

No. The app has no analytics, tracking, advertising, accounts, or network communication. Read the privacy policy for details.
